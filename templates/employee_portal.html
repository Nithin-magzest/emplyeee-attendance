📋 Rules & Regulations Workplace standards every employee must follow
Note: These rules apply to all employees across all departments and levels of the organisation. Violations may lead to warning, suspension, or termination depending on severity.

1. Punctuality & Attendance

  • Employees must report to work on time as per their assigned shift schedule.
  • Habitual late arrivals (more than 3 times in a month) will attract a formal warning.
  • Unauthorised absence without prior approval will result in loss of pay (LOP) for those days.
  • Continuous absence for more than 3 working days without notification may be treated as voluntary abandonment.

2. Dress Code & Personal Appearance

  • Employees must maintain a professional appearance at all times during working hours.
  • Formals or organisation-issued uniforms (where applicable) must be worn on all working days.
  • Casual wear is permitted only on designated casual Fridays or special occasions announced by HR.

3. Workplace Conduct

  • Employees must treat all colleagues, clients, and visitors with respect and professionalism.
  • Aggressive language, shouting, or physical altercations are strictly prohibited.
  • Use of offensive, discriminatory, or abusive language — verbal or written — will result in immediate disciplinary action.
  • Gossiping, spreading rumours, or making false statements about colleagues is not permitted.

4. Use of Office Resources

  • Office equipment, internet, and telephone facilities must be used for official purposes only.
  • Personal use of office resources is limited and must not interfere with work duties.
  • Employees must not download or install unauthorised software on company devices.
  • Misuse or damage to office property will be charged to the responsible employee.

5. Confidentiality

  • Employees must maintain strict confidentiality of all business-sensitive information.
  • Client data, internal strategies, salary details, and employee records must not be disclosed to unauthorised persons.
  • Confidentiality obligations remain in effect even after the termination of employment.

6. Social Media Policy

  • Employees must not post, share, or comment on content that could harm the organisation's reputation on any social media platform.
  • Sharing internal documents, meeting screenshots, or client information on social media is strictly prohibited.
  • Personal social media activities during work hours should not impact productivity.

7. Anti-Corruption & Ethics

  • Bribery, fraud, or corruption in any form is strictly prohibited and will lead to immediate termination and legal action.
  • Employees must declare any conflict of interest to their manager or HR promptly.
  • Accepting gifts worth more than Rs. 500 from vendors or clients must be disclosed to the management.

8. Disciplinary Process

  • Level 1: Verbal warning
  • Level 2: Written warning placed on record
  • Level 3: Suspension without pay
  • Level 4: Termination of employment

The organisation reserves the right to skip steps in cases of serious misconduct.

⚠️ Limitations at Work Boundaries and restrictions applicable to all employees
Important: These limitations exist to ensure a safe, productive, and legally compliant workplace for all. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action.

1. Working Hours

  • Standard working hours are as per the assigned shift. Employees must not extend working hours without prior approval from their manager.
  • Overtime work must be pre-approved and will be compensated as per the organisation's overtime policy.
  • Working more than 12 hours in a single day is not permitted under any circumstances without written approval from HR.

2. Leave Limitations

  • Casual Leave (CL): Maximum 1 day per month (non-accumulative).
  • Sick Leave (SL): Medical certificate is mandatory for sick leave exceeding 2 consecutive days.
  • Earned Leave (EL): Maximum carry-forward as per the organisation's leave policy.
  • Leave cannot be applied retroactively without manager approval.
  • Back-to-back leaves adjoining weekends or holidays require specific approval.

3. Internet & Technology Use

  • Accessing adult, gambling, or any illegal content on office networks or devices is strictly prohibited.
  • Streaming platforms and heavy personal internet usage during working hours are not allowed.
  • Personal devices must not be connected to the organisation's secured internal network without IT approval.
  • Employees must not bypass or attempt to bypass network security measures (firewalls, VPN policies).

4. Client Interaction Limitations

  • Employees must not directly negotiate pricing, contracts, or commitments with clients without authorisation from their manager.
  • Making verbal or written promises to clients outside the approved scope of work is not permitted.
  • All client communication must be documented and archived as per the communication policy.

5. Financial Limitations

  • Expense claims must be submitted within 7 days of incurring the expense with valid receipts.
  • Petty cash usage is limited to pre-approved amounts. Exceeding limits requires written approval.
  • Employees must not make purchases on the organisation's behalf exceeding their authorised spending limit.

6. Workplace Physical Restrictions

  • Access to restricted areas (server rooms, HR records room, management cabins) is only permitted with explicit authorisation.
  • Visitors must be registered at reception and escorted within the premises at all times.
  • Photographs or recordings inside the office premises are not permitted without HR approval.

7. Communication Limitations

  • Official communication must only be sent from the organisation's designated email addresses.
  • Employees must not speak to the media on behalf of the organisation without written approval from management.
  • Internal escalations must follow the designated reporting structure (immediate manager → department head → HR).
📌 Instructions How to use this portal and general workplace guidance
Portal Version: Employee Attendance System — For assistance, raise a Support Ticket through this portal or contact HR directly.

1. Marking Attendance

  • Attendance is marked via face recognition or QR code scan at the office entrance scanner.
  • Check-in must be done within 30 minutes of shift start to avoid being marked Late.
  • You must also mark Check-out before leaving office. Missing check-out will mark you as Half Day.
  • If you face any issue with attendance marking, raise a Support Ticket immediately with the date and reason.

2. Applying for Leave

  • Go to Apply Leave in the sidebar.
  • Select the leave date, type (Casual / Sick / Earned), and provide a reason.
  • Ensure you apply at least 1 day in advance for planned leaves.
  • Emergency leaves (same-day) require you to call or message your manager directly and then apply through the portal.
  • Leave status (Pending / Approved / Rejected) can be tracked under Leave History.

3. Viewing Pay Slips

  • Go to Pay Slips in the sidebar to view and download your monthly salary slips.
  • Pay slips are generated on the 1st of every month for the previous month.
  • If your salary appears incorrect, raise a Support Ticket with details.

4. Support Tickets

  • Use the Support Tickets section to report attendance issues, payroll discrepancies, or any HR-related queries.
  • Provide a clear subject and detailed description to help the admin resolve your ticket faster.
  • Response time is typically within 2 working days.
  • Do not raise duplicate tickets for the same issue.

5. Updating Your Profile

  • Keep your contact number, emergency contact, and bank details updated under My Profile.
  • Changes to critical details (PAN, Aadhar, Bank Account) are subject to HR verification.
  • Profile photo updates must be done through HR directly.

6. Changing Your Password

  • It is recommended to change your portal password every 90 days.
  • Password must be at least 6 characters long.
  • Never share your password with anyone, including IT support staff.
  • If you forget your password, contact the system administrator or HR.

7. Resignation Process

  • Resignation requests must be submitted through the Resignation section with a minimum of 30 days notice (or as per your employment contract).
  • Ensure all handover documents are completed before your last working day.
  • Final settlement and relieving letter will be processed only after proper handover and clearance from all departments.

8. Emergency Contacts

HR Department: Contact your HR manager for any portal or policy-related queries.

IT Support: Raise a Support Ticket for technical issues with the portal.

Payroll: For salary or PF-related queries, raise a Support Ticket with the category "Payroll".

🛡️ POSH Policy Prevention of Sexual Harassment at the Workplace
Legal Mandate: This policy is governed by the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 (POSH Act). Compliance is mandatory for all employees, contractors, and visitors.

1. Purpose & Scope

This policy aims to provide a safe, respectful, and dignified working environment free from sexual harassment. It applies to all employees (permanent, contractual, temporary, interns), clients, customers, and visitors at the workplace and during work-related events, trips, and digital communications.

2. Definition of Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome act or behaviour (whether directly or by implication), such as:

  • Physical contact or advances of a sexual nature
  • Demand or request for sexual favours
  • Making sexually coloured remarks or jokes
  • Showing pornography or objectionable material
  • Unwelcome sexual emails, messages, or social media contact
  • Gender-based insults, intimidation, or threats
  • Stalking (physical or digital)
  • Any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature

3. Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)

The organisation has constituted an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) as mandated by the POSH Act. The committee includes:

  • Presiding Officer: A senior woman employee
  • Internal Members: At least two employees committed to women's welfare
  • External Member: An NGO representative or person familiar with women's issues

Contact the ICC through HR or by raising a Support Ticket marked as POSH Complaint (Confidential).

4. Filing a Complaint

  • A complaint must be filed in writing within 3 months of the incident (extendable in special circumstances).
  • The complaint may be submitted to the Presiding Officer of the ICC or to HR directly.
  • Complaints can be made in writing, by email, or through this portal's Support Ticket system (marked Confidential).
  • Where the aggrieved person is unable to file in writing due to incapacity, the ICC shall render reasonable assistance.

5. Inquiry Process

  • Upon receipt of a complaint, the ICC will commence an inquiry within 7 working days.
  • Both the complainant and the respondent will be given a fair opportunity to present their case.
  • The inquiry will be completed within 90 days of receipt of the complaint.
  • All proceedings of the ICC shall be kept strictly confidential.

6. Interim Relief

During the inquiry, the ICC may recommend interim relief to the complainant such as:

  • Transfer of the aggrieved person or respondent to another department
  • Granting paid leave to the aggrieved person
  • Restraint on the respondent from reporting on the performance of the aggrieved person

7. Consequences of Sexual Harassment

  • Written apology
  • Warning or reprimand placed on record
  • Withholding of promotion or pay increment
  • Deduction from salary as compensation to the aggrieved person
  • Suspension
  • Termination of employment
  • Reporting to law enforcement authorities

8. Protection Against Retaliation

Any employee who retaliates, victimises, or intimidates the complainant or witnesses will face strict disciplinary action, up to and including termination. Retaliation is as serious an offence as the harassment itself.

9. False Complaints

Filing a knowingly false complaint or providing false evidence is also a punishable offence under the Act. However, the inability to prove a complaint does not constitute a false complaint.

10. Awareness & Training

  • All employees are required to complete the mandatory POSH awareness training provided by HR.
  • POSH training sessions are conducted at least once a year.
  • New employees must complete POSH orientation within 30 days of joining.
Zero Tolerance Statement: Our organisation has a zero-tolerance policy towards sexual harassment in any form. Every employee deserves to work in an environment of respect, dignity, and safety. We are committed to maintaining this environment and will take swift, decisive action against any violation of this policy.
